Consortium

A premium digital presence for Winnipeg's private wealth advisory - built to earn trust before the first handshake.
The Opportunity

Winnipeg's wealth market is growing. Most advisory firms haven't kept pace.

Winnipeg's CMA population is expanding, Manitoba's base of private businesses is generating succession and transition demand, and the city's professional sports ecosystem - Jets, Blue Bombers, Sea Bears, Moose, Valour FC - creates a distinct niche for volatile-income planning that no local advisor owns convincingly online.

Consortium has the credentials, the integrated service stack, and the local roots to own this space. What's missing is a digital presence that matches the calibre of the advice. We build that.

Positioning

Winnipeg-first private wealth advisory for families, founders, and athletes navigating complexity.

The website will communicate four proof pillars: credentials-forward advisor profiles with visible designations and specialties, an integrated service stack spanning tax, insurance, business transition, and investment planning, visible local roots with office presence and Manitoba context, and a secure, portal-first client experience that signals professionalism from day one.

The tone throughout: confident, discreet, precise, and relationship-led. No hype. No stock-photo handshakes. Every element earns its place.

Photography & Imagery

All imagery follows a strict editorial direction: warm natural light, muted colour grading that sits within the bronze-navy palette, and compositions that feel candid rather than posed. No stock-photo handshakes. No forced smiles. No clinical lighting. Every image should feel like it was shot for a feature in a private banking journal — atmospheric, unhurried, and quietly confident.

Hero images use full-bleed treatment with a layered gradient overlay (navy to near-black) that anchors text without obscuring atmosphere. Section breaks use landscape crops at 280–360px height. Inline images sit within the content column with subtle 4px rounding.

Voice & Tone

Five words govern every line of copy: confident, discreet, precise, warm, relationship-led.

Sentences are short. Paragraphs breathe. We never oversell — the calibre of the advice does the convincing. Technical terms appear when they earn trust (CFP, CIM, FSWP), never as jargon. The voice speaks to one person across a desk, not an audience in a room.
